# How Much Does Spotify Actually Pay Per Stream? (2026, With Real Numbers)

**The Pulse · The RAAYDR Blog**
*By Ric, Founder of RAAYDR · Last updated July 2026*

Spotify pays artists roughly £0.003 per stream, around a third of a penny, though it never publishes an official rate. We can back that up with real data: one independent artist's distributor dashboard shows $24.6K earned from 6.92 million streams, which works out to about £0.0028 per stream.

Most articles about streaming royalties quote each other. This one quotes a royalty dashboard. RAAYDR was founded by a former session vocalist, and the numbers below come from a real distributor account, screenshot included, so you can check the maths yourself.

## Why is there no official Spotify per-stream rate?

Because Spotify does not pay per stream. It pays rights holders a share of a large revenue pool, divided by total streams across the whole platform. Your "per stream rate" is just the output of that division, and it moves month to month depending on how much money came in and how many streams everyone generated.

Here is the flow in plain English. Spotify collects subscription and advertising revenue, keeps roughly 30%, and pays the rest to rights holders: labels, distributors and publishers. They take their cuts, and whatever remains reaches the artist. If you are signed, your contract decides your slice. If you are independent, you keep most of it after your distributor's fee.

The commonly cited estimate is between $0.003 and $0.005 per stream before splits. Our real-world figure below lands at the bottom of that range.

**Methodology, honestly stated.** This is a blended rate across every store the distributor delivers to, not Spotify alone, and the lifetime earnings figure includes a small amount of download sales, so the true streaming-only rate is fractionally lower. These are earnings paid to the artist account before any collaborator splits. In other words, this is close to a best case for an independent artist.

Nearly seven million streams is not a small catalogue. It is more than most independent artists will ever accumulate. It earned less than one month of the UK average salary per year, spread across a career.

## How much is one listener actually worth on Spotify?

An engaged fan who plays your music around 80 times a month is worth roughly 22 to 24 pence to you. A casual listener who streams you a handful of times a month is worth about a penny.

Sit with that. Your most dedicated listener, someone who plays you daily, someone who would happily buy a ticket and a T-shirt, generates less than the price of a freddo. Not because they do not care, but because the model was never designed to translate their attention into your income.

## Why is the per-stream rate the wrong question?

Because under Spotify's pro-rata model, your fans' subscription money does not go to you. Each subscriber's £11.99 goes into the collective pool and is divided by total platform streams. If your biggest fan listens to you exclusively all month, most of their money still flows to whoever generated the most streams globally that month.

Since 2024 there is also a floor: tracks with fewer than 1,000 streams in a year earn nothing at all, with that money redistributed up the pool. The system structurally favours scale, and punishes exactly the artists building real, deep fanbases.

So the better question is not "how much per stream?" It is "how much of my fans' money reaches me?"

## FAQ

**How much does Spotify pay per 1,000 streams?**
Roughly £3, based on the commonly cited range and the real blended rate of about £0.0028 per stream shown above. The exact figure varies month to month and depends on listener location, subscription type and your splits.

**Does Spotify pay artists directly?**
No. Spotify pays rights holders: labels, distributors and publishers. Independent artists receive their share through a distributor, usually minus a fee or subscription cost. Signed artists receive whatever their contract specifies.

**How many streams do you need to make £1,000 a month?**
At around £0.003 per stream, roughly 330,000 streams every single month. We break that calculation down platform by platform in a separate post.

**Do artists earn anything from tracks with low streams?**
Since early 2024, tracks with fewer than 1,000 streams in a 12-month period generate no royalties on Spotify. That money is redistributed to tracks above the threshold.

**Is the per-stream rate higher on other platforms?**
Generally yes. Apple Music has said it averages around a penny per stream, and several smaller platforms pay more than Spotify. But every major platform still uses a pooled pro-rata model, so the deeper problem, your fans' money not following their listening, remains the same everywhere.
