# How Many Streams Do You Need to Earn £1,000 a Month From Music?

**The Pulse · The RAAYDR Blog**
*By Ric, Founder of RAAYDR · Last updated July 2026*

At Spotify's effective rate of roughly £0.003 per stream, you need about 333,000 streams every single month to earn £1,000. That is around 4 million streams a year, sustained, before any splits with collaborators, labels or distributors.

This post shows the working, platform by platform, using real earnings data rather than recycled estimates. Then it shows a different way to reach the same £1,000.

## How is the streams-to-earnings calculation done?

Divide your target by the per-stream rate. £1,000 divided by £0.003 is 333,333 streams. Our rate comes from a real independent artist's distributor dashboard, $24.6K earned from 6.92 million lifetime streams, which works out to roughly £0.0028 to £0.003 per stream. We published the full dashboard in [our per-stream breakdown](/pulse/how-much-does-spotify-pay-per-stream).

## How many streams do you need for £1,000 on each platform?

Rates for platforms other than Spotify are commonly cited industry estimates, since no platform publishes official figures. The order of magnitude is what matters.

| Platform | Estimated rate per stream | Streams needed for £1,000/month |
| --- | --- | --- |
| YouTube Music | ~£0.0015 | ~665,000 |
| Spotify | ~£0.003 | ~333,000 |
| Amazon Music | ~£0.003 | ~330,000 |
| Apple Music | ~£0.008 | ~128,000 |

Even at Apple Music's friendlier rate, you need well over a hundred thousand plays a month. And remember these are gross figures: a signed artist might keep 20 to 25% of that after the label's share, pushing the real target far higher.

## How many listeners is 333,000 monthly streams?

More than most careers ever reach. If an average listener plays you 5 to 10 times a month, 333,000 streams means somewhere between 33,000 and 66,000 active monthly listeners, month after month, forever. Spotify's own transparency reporting has repeatedly shown that only a small fraction of the artists on the platform reach that scale.

The streaming economy asks you to be huge before it pays you anything like a wage. Since 2024 it does not even pay small: tracks under 1,000 streams a year on Spotify earn nothing at all.

## Is there a way to earn £1,000 a month without mass scale?

Yes: change what gets counted. Under attention-based payment, your income is not streams multiplied by a tiny rate. It is fans multiplied by their attention.

On RAAYDR's founding listener tier, up to £3.50 of each fan's subscription flows to artists every month, split by how much of that fan's listening time each artist earned. So the £1,000 calculation becomes:

| Attention share per fan | Founding fans needed for £1,000/month |
| --- | --- |
| 100% (you are all they play) | ~286 fans |
| 60% | ~476 fans |
| 40% | ~715 fans |
| 25% | ~1,143 fans |

Seven hundred people who genuinely rate you, versus fifty thousand people who vaguely encountered you. Those are different jobs. One of them is achievable for a working independent artist; you can sanity check the numbers yourself with the calculator at [raaydr.com](https://raaydr.com).

## FAQ

**How many streams do you need to earn £100 a month?**
At roughly £0.003 per stream, about 33,000 streams a month. On RAAYDR, roughly 29 founding fans giving you all of their listening, or around 72 fans at a 40% attention share.

**How many Spotify streams equal minimum wage in the UK?**
A full-time UK minimum wage salary is roughly £1,900 a month gross. At £0.003 per stream that is around 630,000 streams every month, or about 7.6 million a year.

**Do these numbers change if I am signed to a label?**
Yes, downward. The rates here reflect what reaches an independent artist's distributor account. Under a typical label deal the artist's royalty is a percentage of that, often 20 to 25%, so the stream targets multiply accordingly.

**Why do different articles quote different per-stream rates?**
Because the rate is an output, not a price. Platforms divide a revenue pool by total streams, so the effective rate shifts with listener location, subscription mix and total platform activity. Any single figure is an average.

**What counts as attention on RAAYDR?**
Listening time. Each subscriber's artist pool is divided among the artists they actually played that month, proportional to their share of that listener's total listening.
